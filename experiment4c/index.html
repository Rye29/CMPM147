Description

Minecraft caves is a tribute to one of the older, yet most influential examples
of proc gen: minecraft. This is meant to represent the old, yet gold generation
of caves in minecraft. Back when I was a kid, minecraft, even before infinite worlds,
was the primary leader in procedurally generated worlds, creating nearly infinite
adventures for us as kids.

Part of this piece's appeal is nostalgia as well as paying respects to what paved the
way for all proc gen users out there: minecraft walked so we could run. Caves used to
be one of the best places to go exploring because it was the one biome that actively
forced you to leave your mark inside it by mining out walls to find coal and ore.
That's exactly what this generator allows you to do: live out the old days of
carving your own path in the untapped underground.

Technical

The base of this generator is based on a perlin noise map, however, I coded a fake erosion
algorithm when ran on each tile checks to see how many blocks next to it are similar to itself.
If itâ€™s not similar enough, it inverts the block type (wall/floor). I took the sum of two of
these erosion layers and it filled any airpockets that stood out too much as well as created larger,
more interesting cave rooms. I used hash values, color caching, and multiplication to dynamically
color the stone parts of the cave, then I added dirt patches with another comparison of hash values,
just like how minecraft scatters dirt throughout its caves sometimes.

Reflection

The final generator of this experiment was the easiest to implement, as the other two provided much challenge through
finding the right algorithms, tweaking algorithms and adjusting gradients. At this point, I had kind of already gotten
a good grip on how to use the rendering engine. However, while uncomplicated by comparison, this generator has a lot of beauty
in its simplicity and its winding compartments.
