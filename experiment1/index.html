Experiment 1 - Art Emporium Spam Email Generator

Description

My intention was to build a simple auto generator that emulated the same programs used by spam ad bots.
I always thought those things were annoying, so I decided to build the thing I hate, just to prove that
anyone could make these things. I recently received a lot of automated rejection emails so that kind of
fueled my remixed creation. I felt that I had to include a few obvious jokes in there so that it didn't
get too cynical. I think everyone who gets annoyed at spam emails would get at least a nasal exhale out
of my take on the text generator.

Technical

One problem I ran into was the action kept changing so I had to cache the generation in a constant order to
keep it consistent. After all, it's meant to emulate a spam email, and at the very least, the hobby needs to
be consistent. Working in HTML was a little slow when just pushing and publishing to a remote server, so I
installed the Live Preview extension to help speed things up.

Reflection

Majority of the backbone of this project was all Professor Wes Modes in the technical part of the generator since
they provided the template. I worked on this experiment on my own besides the Professor providing the base resources
since I believed that it was important that I be able to support myself in a project setting before supporting others.
I have worked with glitch in high school and this was a great refresher as well as a good way to learn how to transfer
my work into a proper portfolio.

Documentation is a little annoying, but I understand the importance of it. It shows what the developers were trying to
do and what they were thinking. It’s also giving me a good retrospective view on how I could improve my workflow, for
example I could’ve sat down and planned out when to do this assignment better because I kind of sat down for two hours,
did the glitch project and initial transfer in one session and now I’m writing the reflection after debugging in the
second. Other than that, I feel like I did a decent job on this intro experiment.
