Experiment 3 - Alternate Worlds

Description

Using autotiler logic, procedural noise, and p5js, I created a dungeon room
generator and a desert map generator that procedurally builds using the autotiler
and a generated seed. The dungeon was inspired by Legend of Zelda and Castlevania
while the primary inspiration of the desert was Dune (2021)

Technical

For both generators, I used p5's noise() function to generate the overlay effects and
a mix of sin(), seconds() and millis() to move them across the screen. The Dungeon generator
works by connecting abstract cubes together to create a unique room shape. The desert generator
uses perlin noise to generate a representation of a topological map with the rounded up peaks being
represented by mountains and darker sand representing the deepest points

Reflection

I’ll cut straight to the point: I think this is more of a solid A than A plus work from me. While I
did fulfill the assignment requirements and then some, I feel like I did that extra work for that
extra point because I felt like my work wasn’t good enough. Although it is a generator and it’s
not going to be perfect, I think that I just have trouble accepting that as a 3D artist. Even though
at the end of the day, it will never be perfect, and it doesn’t need to be. The other students that
have produced some really great stuff have actually complimented my work in Discord so at the
end of the day, maybe it’s just me being my own worst critic.

I feel that what I did well in this experiment was quickly implementing the logic side
of the generator: creating autotilers, getting around HTML’s quirks, and making convincing overworlds
and dungeons. I thought that the logic, while flawed in very specific seeds, ended up being the
peak of the experiment. I only wish I could’ve come up with something more creative rather than
clouds and fog for the living part. What I originally wanted to do for the overworld was a heat
haze effect but that would’ve required shader knowledge that I do not have.
